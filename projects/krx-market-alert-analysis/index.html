Effectiveness Analysis of the KRX Market Alert System

A statistical analysis project using 18,384 daily records of KRX warning-listed securities to examine market instability and structural changes.

PythonSQLpandasstatsmodelsJoinpoint RegressionQuantile RegressionTime-Series Analysis
Period
Jan. 2025 – Jul. 2025
Role
Independent research: data collection, statistical analysis, interpretation, and thesis writing
Type
Master’s Thesis

Problem Context

The research examined whether market alert signals were associated with changes in downside risk and instability patterns in listed securities.

What I Did

  • Collected and structured 18,384 daily observations of warning-listed securities.
  • Measured volatility and crash-risk patterns using NCSKEW and DUVOL indicators.
  • Applied Joinpoint Regression and Quantile Regression to identify structural shifts and distribution-level effects.
  • Organized the analysis into a reporting structure that could be interpreted for practical risk monitoring.

Outcome and Portfolio Value

  • Built a full-cycle research workflow from raw financial data to statistical interpretation.
  • Strengthened the ability to translate statistical outputs into domain-specific risk explanations.
  • Created a finance-domain project that supports applications for data, risk, and analytics roles.
